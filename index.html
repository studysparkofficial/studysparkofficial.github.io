What is Thermodynamics?

Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that deals with heat and temperature, and their relation to energy and work.

Important Laws:

  • Zeroth Law: If A = B and B = C, then A = C.
  • First Law: Energy can neither be created nor destroyed.
  • Second Law: Heat cannot spontaneously flow from cold to hot body.
  • Third Law: Entropy approaches a constant value as temperature approaches absolute zero.
